## Q16 — SBC / economic-FCF methodology: is reported FCF a "mirage"?

### The competing lenses

The report asserts: *economic FCF = reported FCF (+$108M) − full grant-date SBC ($354M) = −$246M*, and uses this to swing capital allocation to FAIL.

There are three defensible frameworks; the report uses the most punitive one without acknowledging the alternatives:

1. **Full grant-date SBC subtraction (report's lens, "−$246M").** Treats every dollar of grant-date SBC as an equivalent cash cost to equity holders. *Strength:* SBC is unambiguously a real compensation expense (employees would demand cash otherwise) and is non-discretionary. *Weakness:* it double-counts relative to dilution if no buyback occurs — the cost to existing holders is the *dilution*, which is a function of the share price at issuance and forfeitures, not the grant-date fair value. Grant-date SBC and actual dilution diverge materially when the stock moves.

2. **Dilution-at-market, net of forfeitures (Damodaran's preferred lens).** The true cost to existing shareholders of un-bought-back SBC is the value of the *new shares actually issued* (net of forfeitures and net of option-exercise proceeds), valued at market. Damodaran's standard treatment: SBC is a real expense that should reduce earnings/FCFE, but the cleaner per-share approach is to (a) expense SBC in the income statement AND (b) track diluted share growth, then value on a per-share basis — *not* to both subtract grant-date SBC from FCF *and* dilute the share count (that is the double-count).

3. **SBC as a real, recurring, non-cash compensation expense (accounting/GAAP lens).** SBC reduces GAAP operating income (Natera's FY2025 GAAP op loss was −$310M, *inclusive* of $354M SBC). On this lens, "FCF positive but GAAP loss-making" is simply the standard cash-vs-accrual gap, and the honest single-number summary is: *Natera is GAAP-unprofitable, generates modest reported FCF, and funds ~15% of revenue of compensation with equity that dilutes ~4-5%/yr net.*

### The defensible framework

**For a per-share-IRR underwriter, the correct discipline is option 2 (dilution-at-market) applied per share — NOT option 1 layered on top of share-count dilution.** Doing both is double-counting. The honest statement is:

- Reported FCF (+$108M FY2025) is **real cash** and is not, by itself, a "mirage." Natera genuinely turned cash-flow positive.
- BUT that FCF accrues to a **share base growing ~10–11%/yr historically** (91M FY2021 → 137M FY2025 diluted weighted-avg; ~11% CAGR), of which roughly half is recurring SBC and half was the one-time 2024 convert conversion.
- So the per-share economic reality: **positive but heavily diluted FCF.** The "−$246M economic FCF" number is a useful *rhetorical* shock but is technically an over-subtraction if you ALSO model dilution. The rigorous version is: value FCF per share and grow the share count, OR subtract net-dilution-at-market from FCF and hold shares flat — pick one, not both.

### Per-share FCF sensitivity (Q16 model)

Assumptions: 2030 FCF scenarios from the existing files (base $2.0B, bull $2.8B, bear-light $0.85B). Starting share count ~140M (mid-2026). Two paths: (i) continued ~4.5%/yr net dilution, no buyback; (ii) a $400M/yr buyback (≈ enough to absorb SBC) that holds shares roughly flat to slightly down. Buyback assumed executed at an average ~$220/share (4,500K → ~1.8M shares/yr retired at $220, partially offsetting ~6M/yr SBC issuance).

| Scenario (2030) | 2030 FCF | Shares 2030, NO buyback (~4.5%/yr dilution) | FCF/share (no buyback) | Shares 2030, $400M/yr buyback (~flat) | FCF/share (with buyback) | Per-share uplift from buyback |
|---|---|---|---|---|---|---|
| Bull | $2.8B | ~175M | $16.00 | ~143M | $19.58 | +22% |
| Base | $2.0B | ~175M | $11.43 | ~143M | $13.99 | +22% |
| Bear-light | $0.85B | ~175M | $4.86 | ~143M | $5.94 | +22% |

*(No-buyback share count: 140M × 1.045^4.5 ≈ 175M by 2030. With-buyback: SBC issuance ~6M/yr offset by ~5–6M/yr repurchased at ~$220 → roughly flat at ~143M. Uplift is ~22% to per-share FCF across all scenarios — a clean illustration of why a buyback sized to SBC matters to per-share IRR.)*

**Interpretation.** A $400M/yr buyback adds ~22% to 2030 per-share FCF in every scenario — i.e., it converts ~4.5%/yr of dilution drag into per-share accretion. This is exactly the "absorb SBC dilution" engagement ask the report makes, and the model shows it is worth ~3–5%/yr to equity-holder IRR. That validates the *direction* of the report's critique even though its specific "−$246M" number is methodologically loose.

### Honest verdict on the "mirage" claim

**The truth is more nuanced than "FCF is a mirage."**

- Reported FCF is **real cash** (not a mirage) — Natera genuinely inflected cash-flow positive and the 2024 breakeven commitment was met.
- **BUT** per-share value creation is materially compressed by ~4.5–5%/yr net dilution, and the company funds 15.4% of revenue in equity comp with **zero offsetting buyback**, so equity-holder economics lag business FCF growth by ~3–5%/yr.
- The report's "−$246M economic FCF" is a **defensible-but-aggressive rhetorical device** that double-counts when paired with explicit dilution modeling. The cleaner, equally damning framing: *"Natera's reported +$108M FCF accrues to a share base growing ~5%/yr, with SBC at 15.4% of revenue and no buyback — so per-share FCF is positive but structurally diluted, and a $400M/yr buyback would lift per-share FCF ~22%."*

**Recommendation: keep the capital-allocation criticism, but re-state the SBC math as per-share dilution drag (option 2), not as a −$246M absolute "economic FCF" (option 1).** The conclusion (capital-allocation policy is sub-par; a buyback is the obvious fix) survives intact and is actually *more* rigorous stated this way. Confidence: **High** on framework; **High** on share-count data; SBC dollar figures for 2021–2023 are estimates flagged as such.

## Q17 — Litigation status (MATERIAL CORRECTIONS to prior files)

### (1) Prospera / CareDx Lanham Act false-advertising judgment — **PRIOR FILES ARE WRONG ON THE OUTCOME**

The report (04-risks §A; final §5) states the March 2022 jury verdict (Natera "intentionally and willfully" false-advertised Prospera) was "upheld by district court July 2023; injunction issued" and treats it as a standing adverse finding. **The appellate outcome reverses the damages.**

**Third Circuit, CareDx v. Natera, No. 23-2427, decided Aug 28, 2025** (Judge Patty Shwartz):
- **Liability AFFIRMED:** "The District Court correctly concluded that there is sufficient evidence for a reasonable juror to find the challenged claims were literally false." The false-advertising finding stands.
- **$45M damages VACATED:** the ~$21.2M compensatory + ~$23.7M punitive award was erased because CareDx **failed to prove causation/injury** (could not show Natera's false statements actually deceived consumers and caused CareDx harm).
- **Injunction STANDS:** Natera remains barred from the specific false superiority claims.
- **Supreme Court:** CareDx filed a cert petition (Feb 2026); SCOTUS requested Natera's response (Feb 2026); Natera filed opposition (~Apr 2026) arguing the claimed circuit split is "imagined." **Cert decision pending as of late May 2026** (no grant/denial yet).

**Corrected character read:** The *finding of literal falsity* against Natera is real and affirmed on appeal — the character flag is NOT erased. **But the $45M monetary exposure is gone**, and the injunction is the only live remedy. For an underwriter: the reputational/character point survives (an appellate court affirmed Natera made literally false superiority claims), but there is **no material dollar liability** here. The report's "upheld... injunction issued" should be corrected to "**liability affirmed on appeal Aug 2025, damages vacated for lack of causation, injunction stands, cert petition pending.**" Confidence: **High.**

### (1b) RELATED CORRECTION — the $96M patent verdict

02-moat / 03-reinvestment state Natera "received $96M and an ongoing royalty" from CareDx after a January 2024 patent win, treating it as a Prospera moat asset. **A federal district court OVERTURNED the $96M jury verdict in 2025** (GenomeWeb, CareDx 8-K: "District Court Reverses Jury Decision in Patent Infringement Case Overturning Prior $96 Million Verdict"). The "$96M + perpetual royalty to Natera" claim in the moat files is **no longer accurate** and should be downgraded — the patent-royalty Prospera-moat asset is now in doubt / on appeal. Confidence: **High** that the verdict was overturned; **Medium** on the current appeal posture. *This weakens 02-moat §D and 03-reinvestment's Prospera revenue tail.*

### (2) 2022 securities class action — ONGOING, no settlement, class certified, on interlocutory appeal

Veracyte (profitable, ~15% growth) sits at ~5×. **Nothing in the scaled-dx peer set sustains a double-digit EV/revenue multiple once growth slows below ~25%.**

### Implication for the report's exit multiples and buy trigger

The final-report uses **22–28× FCF exit multiples** and **~10× EV/revenue** in its 2030 bridges, asserted without grounding.

- **22–28× FCF is RICH but defensible *only* if Natera is still growing 20%+ and ~30% FCF-margin at the 2030 exit.** A 25× FCF multiple ≈ 4% FCF yield ≈ roughly a mature-compounder multiple. For a dx franchise, the base rate suggests the market would more likely assign **~15–22× FCF (EV/EBITDA ~12–18×)** to a slowing-but-profitable Natera — i.e., the report's 22–28× sits at the **optimistic top** of the empirical band. The **bear-light 18× is the right anchor; the base-case 22× is reasonable; the bull 28× is aggressive and would require sustained 20%+ growth at exit.**
- **On EV/revenue:** Natera at **12× today is ~2.2× the peer median (5.4×)** and only matched by still-hyped, unprofitable Guardant/Tempus. The matured analogs (Exact ~4×, Veracyte ~5×) imply that *as Natera's growth decelerates, multiple compression alone is a major headwind* — exactly the risk a 5-year underwriter must price. If Natera re-rates to even 7× 2030 revenue (generous vs. Exact's 4×), the implied EV on $7B 2030 revenue is ~$49B — supportive of the base case, but the *path* runs through multiple compression that the bull bridge understates.

**Does this defend the $140–150 buy trigger?** **Largely yes, and arguably the trigger should be a touch lower.** The buy trigger is built to deliver ~15%+ IRR under the base case with bounded downside. Grounding the exit multiple at the *empirical* 18–22× FCF (rather than 25–28×) makes the math *more conservative*, which pushes fair value *down*, reinforcing that ~$140–150 (~$20B market cap, ~7× 2030 revenue / ~18–20× 2030 FCF) is the right zone for asymmetry to flip favorable. **The base-rate evidence supports the buy trigger but undercuts the upper end (28×) of the bull exit multiple** — the bull case at $420 implied value should be haircut for realistic multiple compression. Confidence: **High** on the empirical ranges; **Medium-High** on the IRR implication.

**Net Q18:** Scaled, slowing dx franchises trade at **~4–7× EV/revenue and ~12–20× EV/EBITDA/FCF at maturity**, not the 10× revenue / 22–28× FCF the report's bull/base bridges lean on. Natera's current 12× revenue is a peak-of-cycle, top-of-peer multiple matched only by unprofitable narrative names. The $140–150 buy trigger is **defensible and conservatively re-grounded**; the report's higher exit multiples (especially 28×) are **optimistic vs. base rates** and should be flagged.
