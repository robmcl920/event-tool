## 2. Product portfolio

Natera operates as a **single reporting segment** (molecular testing services) but markets its products across three "areas of healthcare": **women's health, oncology, and organ health**, plus a software/cloud product (Constellation) for international partners. The 10-K explicitly states the company "applies its proprietary technology in the fields of women's health, oncology and organ health."

### 2a. Panorama — non-invasive prenatal screening (NIPT)
- **Clinical question:** Does the fetus carry common aneuploidies (Down syndrome [T21], Edwards [T18], Patau [T13], sex-chromosome abnormalities) or one of five microdeletions; also fetal RhD status. SNP-based methodology distinguishes maternal vs. fetal cfDNA, enabling unique abilities such as detecting triploidy and identifying twin zygosity.
- **Workflow:** Maternal blood draw → cfDNA isolation → targeted SNP sequencing → bioinformatic SNP allele ratio analysis. Performed in U.S. CLIA labs; turnaround typically 7–10 days.
- **Target population:** All pregnant women (now offered to average-risk pregnancies after ACOG/SMFM guidance shift).
- **ASP:** Approximately **$400+** as of 2025 (up from ~$250 historically); contracted rates can be in the $850–$900 range, but realized ASP is lower due to payer mix and bad debt.
- **Launch:** 2013. Natera describes Panorama as the U.S. market leader by volume.

### 2b. Horizon — carrier screening
- **Clinical question:** Is a prospective parent (or couple) a carrier for autosomal-recessive or X-linked conditions (cystic fibrosis, DMD, SMA, Fragile X, etc.)?
- **Workflow:** Blood or saliva sample → NGS targeted panel → carrier/heterozygote report. Panel sizes are customizable.
- **Output:** The 10-K describes coverage for up to **835 inherited conditions**.
- **Target population:** Reproductive-age men and women, particularly pre-conception and early pregnancy.
- **ASP:** Not separately disclosed; Natera bundles women's health ASP commentary.
- **Launch:** 2012.

### 2c. Empower — hereditary cancer screening
- **Clinical question:** Does an individual carry a germline pathogenic variant predisposing to cancer (BRCA1/2, Lynch syndrome genes, etc.)?
- **Workflow:** Saliva or blood → NGS panel covering up to 81 genes across 12+ cancer types (5 panel options) with optional customization to ~190+ genes.
- **Target population:** Patients with personal/family cancer history; increasingly tested alongside Signatera at oncology referral.
- **ASP:** Self-pay cash price $249. Insured/contracted reimbursement varies.
- **Launch:** 2020.

### 2d. Signatera — molecular residual disease (MRD) — *the crown jewel*

**Clinical question:** Is residual / recurrent cancer present after curative-intent treatment, and is the patient at risk of relapse?

**Technology:**
- **Tumor-informed, personalized assay.** After surgery, the patient's tumor tissue and matched normal blood undergo **whole-exome sequencing (WES)**. A proprietary algorithm selects ~16 clonal somatic mutations unique to that patient's tumor.
- A **bespoke multiplex-PCR panel** is then built and used on subsequent plasma samples to look for circulating tumor DNA at sub-0.01% allele frequency.
- The tumor-informed design (a) filters out clonal hematopoiesis (CHIP) variants that confound tumor-naïve assays and (b) enables higher sensitivity at low ctDNA levels than off-the-shelf panels.

**Workflow:**
- **First test design:** 3–4 weeks after receipt of tumor + blood.
- **Subsequent monitoring blood draws:** 7–10 day TAT; 6 mL EDTA + Streck tubes.
- Repeat blood draws typically every 3 months (or more frequently in early adjuvant period).
- Output: binary positive/negative ctDNA result with quantitation; PPV for relapse cited at >98%.
- Phlebotomy: clinic on-site or via Natera's mobile phlebotomy service.

**Indications and Medicare coverage (LCD L38779):**
- Colorectal cancer (Stage II–IV adjuvant/recurrence monitoring) — first MolDx coverage 2020
- Breast cancer (neoadjuvant + adjuvant)
- Muscle-invasive bladder cancer
- Stage I–III NSCLC
- Ovarian / fallopian tube / peritoneal cancer
- Immune-checkpoint inhibitor therapy response monitoring (pan-tumor)
- Signatera Genome (a higher-resolution variant) also has Medicare coverage as of late 2025.

**Patients served:** >300,000 to date (Natera product page).

**ASP:**
- ~$1,200 in Q3 2025 → **~$1,225 in Q4 2025**, with management guiding to ~$1,275 exiting 2026 and a long-term internal target of ~**$2,000**. Medicare ADLT rate (>$3,000 per test) is well above realized ASP due to commercial-payer drag and bad debt.

**Volumes:**
- 2024 oncology tests: 528,200 (incl. ~498,300 clinical MRD).
- 2025 oncology tests: **800,800** (+51.6% YoY); clinical MRD 769,700 (+54% YoY).
- Q1 2026 oncology tests: **258,900** (+54.4% YoY); clinical MRD ~249,000.
- CEO Chapman (Q4 2025 call): "more than 50% of oncologists in the United States ordered a Signatera test in the quarter."

**Publications:** "over 170 peer-reviewed publications as of December 31, 2025" (FY2025 10-K). Total Natera publications: >350.

**Launch:** Research-use 2017; clinical CLIA launch 2019.

**Latitude:** A complementary blood-only (tumor-naïve) MRD test for colorectal cancer, for cases where tumor tissue is unavailable. Far smaller commercial footprint than Signatera.

### 2e. Altera — comprehensive tumor genomic profiling (CGP)
- **Clinical question:** Which therapies (targeted or immunotherapy) are best matched to this solid tumor's mutational profile? Also reports MSI and TMB.
- **Workflow:** FFPE tumor tissue + matched normal blood → whole-exome + transcriptome sequencing with >500× boosted coverage over 440 medically-important genes.
- **Target population:** Patients with advanced solid tumors for therapy selection or trial matching. Often bundled with Signatera in the Natera oncology offering.
- **Market:** Natera estimates the standalone CGP market at ~$6B (competing with Foundation Medicine, Caris, Tempus, Guardant).
- **Launch:** 2021.

### 2f. Prospera — organ transplant rejection
- **Clinical question:** Is a transplanted organ being rejected (subclinical TCMR or ABMR)?
- **Technology:** Detects **donor-derived cell-free DNA (dd-cfDNA)** in recipient blood via the same SNP backbone Natera uses for NIPT (the SNPs that distinguish donor vs. recipient). The "Quantification" feature added in recent years provides absolute dd-cfDNA quantitation in addition to fraction.
- **Indications:** Kidney, heart, and lung — all three have Medicare coverage. Active multi-center trials (ProActive registry for kidney; ACES EMB heart trial completed enrollment, readout mid-2027).
- **Workflow:** Blood draw; routine surveillance schedule.
- **ASP:** Medicare reimbursement rate ~$2,840 (March 2020 LCD pricing); realized ASP not separately disclosed.
- **Launch:** 2019 (Prospera Kidney); subsequent heart and lung indications added.
- **2025 commentary:** Management noted Prospera growing >50% YoY in Q4 2025.

### 2g. Renasight — chronic kidney disease genetic panel
- **Clinical question:** Is there a genetic cause for this patient's kidney disease?
- **Workflow:** Saliva or blood NGS panel covering **397 genes**.
- **Target population:** CKD patients, transplant candidates, family screening. Cross-sells naturally to nephrologists who already use Prospera.
- **Launch:** 2020.

### 2h. Vistara & Fetal Focus — single-gene NIPT
- **Clinical question:** Does the fetus carry a de-novo dominant single-gene disorder (skeletal dysplasias, Noonan syndrome, etc.) — conditions usually missed by aneuploidy NIPT?
- **Vistara:** 25 conditions, CE Marked.
- **Fetal Focus:** Newer (2025) panel covering 21 conditions; expanded in Q3 2025 from 5 to 20 genes.

### 2i. Spectrum PGT — pre-implantation genetic testing
- For IVF embryos prior to implantation, screens for chromosomal aneuploidy and single-gene disorders.
- **Launch:** 2009 (originally one of Natera's first products).

### 2j. Anora — products of conception (POC)
- Analyzes miscarriage tissue to identify chromosomal cause of pregnancy loss; SNP-microarray-based.
- **Launch:** 2010.

### 2k. Constellation — cloud informatics platform
- SaaS/licensing offering enabling international labs and partners to run Natera's bioinformatics pipelines on their own NGS instruments. CE Marked. Revenue is recorded under "licensing and other revenues" (~$10M in 2025).

### 2l. CLARITY / PhasED-Seq (from December 2025 Foresight Diagnostics acquisition)
- **Phased-variant** ultrasensitive ctDNA technology (LOD95 of 0.3 ppm; can detect <0.1 ppm). Foresight's **CLARITY** assay is used in NCCN-cited DLBCL/lymphoma trials.
- Acquired for **$275M upfront + up to $175M earnouts (all-stock)** on December 5, 2025.
- Integration into Signatera underway; clinical launch of phased-variant Signatera expected 2026.

## 4. Technology platform

### 4a. Core technology — cfDNA + SNP-based detection

**Cell-free DNA (cfDNA)** is short (~165 bp) DNA fragments that float in plasma after release from dying cells. In pregnancy, fetal/placental cfDNA mixes with maternal cfDNA; in cancer, tumor cells shed ctDNA into blood; in transplant, the donor organ sheds dd-cfDNA distinguishable by genotype.

Natera's signature innovation is using **single-nucleotide polymorphisms (SNPs)** rather than counting reads to make calls:
- For NIPT (Panorama), the algorithm compares allele ratios at thousands of polymorphic SNPs to distinguish fetal vs. maternal DNA contribution and infer fetal aneuploidy, triploidy, and parental origin.
- For Prospera, the same SNP framework distinguishes donor vs. recipient cfDNA (because donor and recipient have different genotypes).
- For Signatera, the personalized PCR panel targets **patient-specific somatic SNVs** identified in the tumor — a different paradigm but the same "exploit SNP / variant differences" intellectual lineage.

The over-arching technical philosophy: **target informative loci rather than perform broad shallow sequencing.**

### 4b. Tumor-informed vs. tumor-naïve MRD

Two competing MRD approaches in oncology:
- **Tumor-naïve / tumor-uninformed** (e.g., Guardant Reveal, certain CGP-based MRD assays): use a fixed panel of recurrently mutated genes and look for any of those in plasma. Pros: no tumor tissue needed. Cons: lower sensitivity, susceptible to clonal hematopoiesis false positives.
- **Tumor-informed** (Signatera): WES the tumor, build a custom assay for that patient. Pros: higher sensitivity at lower ctDNA fractions; CHIP variants filtered out; high PPV. Cons: requires tumor tissue, 3–4 week assay-design lead time, more labor per case.

Signatera is the **commercial and clinical leader in tumor-informed MRD** with >770K tests run in 2025 and >170 peer-reviewed publications. The Foresight (PhasED-Seq) acquisition adds **phased-variant** detection to push LOD into the sub-ppm range — further widening the sensitivity moat.

### 4c. Bioinformatics / informatics pipeline

- Proprietary informatics pipeline ("Constellation" being the externalized SaaS layer for partners).
- The 10-K notes "molecular assays measuring informative regions across the genome" combined with "statistical algorithms incorporating broader scientific community data."
- IP portfolio: **>650 issued or pending U.S. and foreign patents** as of YE 2025.
- "Panorama AI" upgrade in 2021 reduced COGS and improved performance.

### 4d. Lab infrastructure

- Three CLIA-certified labs (Austin TX, San Carlos CA, Boulder CO).
- Annualized throughput tracking ~4M tests/yr (Q1 2026 run-rate >4M).
- Automation has driven gross margin from ~41% (2022) to ~67% (Q4 2025).

### 4e. Platform reusability

The same core stack (cfDNA isolation + SNP-based methods + Constellation informatics + sales infrastructure) has been re-applied across:
- Reproductive (Panorama, Horizon, Spectrum, Anora, Vistara, Fetal Focus)
- Oncology (Signatera, Altera, Empower, Latitude, future phased-variant MRD)
- Transplant (Prospera kidney/heart/lung)
- Nephrology (Renasight)

R&D leverage is high — new indications largely re-use existing assay chemistry and lab capacity. This matters enormously for reinvestment analysis (Stage 3).

## 5. Customers and go-to-market

- **Who orders the tests:** OB/GYNs and maternal-fetal medicine specialists (women's health); medical oncologists and surgical oncologists (Signatera, Altera, Empower); nephrologists and transplant centers (Prospera, Renasight); reproductive endocrinologists / IVF clinics (Spectrum).
- **Sales force:** Direct U.S. sales force is the primary commercial engine. Natera does not publicly disclose exact rep counts; commentary on Q4 2025 call indicated substantial 2025 build-out, particularly in oncology, with SG&A flat sequentially in H2 2025 as the team digested expansion. Industry estimates put the combined U.S. sales organization in the high hundreds to ~1,000+ reps spread across the three franchises.
- **Who pays:**
  - **Medicare** (foundational — Signatera ADLT >$3,000; Prospera ~$2,840; Panorama covered as advanced screening)
  - **Commercial payers** (UnitedHealthcare, Cigna, Anthem, BCBS plans — most major plans are in-network for Empower, expanding coverage for Signatera)
  - **State Medicaid programs**
  - **Patient self-pay** (with financial assistance programs)
- **International:** Distributor / lab-partner model; >100 lab partners in >80 countries (per 10-K excerpt). Constellation cloud SaaS licenses inform clinical workflows abroad.
- **Customer concentration:** Diffuse — no single end-customer accounts for a material % of revenue. Payer concentration (Medicare ADLT for Signatera; commercial in-network contracts) is more relevant than ordering-provider concentration.
